Sekuwa's Recipe

Description

Sekuwa is a popular Nepalese dish made of marinated meat, typically chicken or goat, skewered and grilled to perfection. The meat is marinated in a blend of spices and herbs, giving it a rich flavor and tender texture. Sekuwa is often served as a snack or as part of a main meal, accompanied by fresh salad and dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • Boneless meat (chicken or goat)
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Green chilies
  • Cumin powder
  • Coriander powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Red chili powder
  • Salt
  • Vegetable oil
  • Lemon juice

Steps

  1. Cut the boneless meat into bite-sized pieces and set aside.
  2. In a blender, combine garlic, ginger, green chilies, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, vegetable oil, and lemon juice to make a smooth marinade.
  3. Marinate the meat pieces in the prepared marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Thread the marinated meat pieces onto skewers, alternating with pieces of onion, bell peppers, and tomatoes if desired.
  5. Preheat a grill to medium-high heat. Grill the skewers for about 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and has charred grill marks.
  6. Remove the skewers from the grill and serve hot sekuwa with fresh salad and dipping sauce.
